Forests in flames

Antecedente 4

It’s natural to think of satellite images as photographs from space. However, they are fundamentally different to regular pictures. A camera focuses visible light through the lens and captures it on a light-sensitive surface.

A satellite uses instruments to measure multiple types of light, some invisible to the human eye, and then represents the data as an image. This means we are able to select combinations of this data and put them into coloured images, which sometimes tell us much more than how an area looks to the naked eye.
